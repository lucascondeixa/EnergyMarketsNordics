What it does

The optimiser solves a 7-day rolling Mixed Integer Linear Programme (MILP) across a 168-hour horizon at hourly resolution. For each run it produces:

FI Zone

  • Nuclear unit commitment (start/stop, min run times, ramp limits, planned outages)
  • Hydro reservoir scheduling (inflow, storage dynamics, dispatch timing)
  • Wind must-take allocation
  • FCR-N ancillary service capacity bids (Fingrid frequency reserve)

SE2 Zone

  • Pump storage arbitrage (buy cheap, sell high within reservoir limits)
  • Round-trip efficiency and ramp constraints applied

Output

  • Hourly dispatch schedule (CSV)
  • 10-step Elspot bid curves (CSV, ready for manual submission)
  • Revenue breakdown — cash generation vs. planning credits

Key assumptions

  • Hydro dispatch threshold — 61 EUR/MWh. The terminal water value is set at 55 EUR/MWh (÷ 0.90 turbine efficiency = 61 EUR/MWh marginal cost). The optimiser only dispatches hydro when the Elspot price exceeds this threshold. If market prices shift significantly, this parameter should be reviewed.
  • Reservoir initial fill — 85% (425 GWh of 500 GWh). Reflects post-spring-flood high-water conditions in June. Should be updated from actual SCADA data before use in other seasons.
  • Kemijoki Oy modelled as a single aggregated block (630 MW / 480 GWh). Fortum's 57% share of the ~20-plant Kemijoki river fleet is represented as one combined turbine and reservoir using public capacity estimates. Individual plant cascades, inter-reservoir constraints, and plant-specific inflow profiles are not captured — these would require Kemijoki SCADA data.
  • Single aggregated FI reservoir. Vuoksi and Oulujoki are modelled as one combined reservoir (500 GWh, Oulujärvi). In reality they are separate river systems with correlated but distinct spring flood timing.
  • Price forecast — seasonal median + level correction. Beyond confirmed D+1 ENTSO-E prices (~12 real hours), the horizon is filled using a 90-day historical median profile adjusted to the last 7 days of actual prices. This captures the current price regime but not short-term fundamental drivers (hydro balance, wind outlook, cross-border flows).
  • FCR-N only for ancillary services. FCR-D, aFRR, and mFRR are modelled in the config but not yet enabled. FCR-N is currently the highest-value ancillary service for flexible hydro in Finland.
  • Wind is must-take. Pjelax wind output is treated as a non-dispatchable input — always dispatched at floor price. Curtailment is not modelled.
  • SE2 reservoir sizes are stylised. Kymmen, Letten, and Eggsjön reservoir capacities are estimates. Replace with Fortum SCADA data for production use.
